Bimtal Lake:

Bhimtal Lake, nestled in the picturesque town of Bhimtal in Uttarakhand, India, is a serene freshwater lake surrounded by lush greenery and hills. It offers boating, kayaking, and breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The lake is adorned with a small island housing an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

How To Reach it:

By Air:

The nearest airport is Pantnagar Airport, approximately 58 kilometers away. From there, travelers can hire a taxi or use public transportation to reach Bhimtal.

By Train:

The nearest railway station is Kathgodam Railway Station, around 22 kilometers from Bhimtal. From Kathgodam, taxis and buses are available to reach Bhimtal.

History:

Bhimtal Lake, named after Bhima from the Mahabharata, is believed to be formed by Bhima's digging.
